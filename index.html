BSI Lab explores how skilled biological actions can be captured, represented, transferred, and reproduced through wearable sensors, haptics, robotics, and embodied intelligence.

Vision

Research Vision

Biological skills are not merely movements. They are accumulated intelligence shaped by bodies, tools, environments, and experience. BSI Lab aims to transform these skills into measurable, transferable, and reproducible forms of machine intelligence.
Research

Research Areas

01

Biological Skill Sensing

Wearable sensors for measuring human and biological movements. FMG, MMG, EMG, tactile, pressure, and motion sensing.

02

Skill Representation

Modeling skilled actions as structured data. Multimodal skill datasets. Foundation models for biological skill intelligence.

03

Skill Transfer

Teleoperation. Haptic feedback. Human-to-human and human-to-robot skill transfer across domains and platforms.

04

Skill Embodiment

Robot hand control. Humanoid manipulation. Reproduction of expert skills in robotic systems through learned representations.

05

Human Augmentation

Wearable interfaces. Assistive robotics. Physical-digital interaction for enhancing human capability and performance.

Principal Investigator

Hanbit Jin, Ph.D.

Senior Researcher, Electronics and Telecommunications Research Institute (ETRI)

Hanbit Jin is a senior researcher at ETRI working at the intersection of wearable healthcare, haptics, flexible sensors, and robotics. His long-term vision is to build technologies that preserve, transfer, and reproduce valuable biological skills for future embodied intelligent systems.
